Experience



GANGARAMAYA TEMPLE

VISIT THE 120-YEAR-OLD BUDDHIST TEMPLE


Located 2 kms away from Granbell Hotel, Gangaramaya Temple is the most sacred and iconic temple within Colombo’s city limits. Admired for its unique architecture which plays a clever mix of Sri Lankan, Chinese, Indian and Thai styles, the revered complex consists of the main temple, a museum and library and a vocational training centre.


The temple is nestled in proximity to the Beira Lake in Colombo 2, where you’ll also find the temple’s meditation pavilion perched on the emerald waters. When visiting the Buddhist place of worship, it is imperative to ensure you are dressed modestly, covering your knees and shoulders. Footwear must also be kept outside before entering the premises.


To Gangaramaya Temple – 5 minutes drive.

LOTUS TOWER

THE ISLAND’S TALLEST TOWER


Located almost 4 km away from Granbell Hotel, the Lotus Tower was built to be Sri Lanka’s tallest tower standing at a height of 350 m. The symbolic structure is the second tallest tower in South Asia, the 11th tallest in Asia and the 19th tallest tower in the world.


It was designed and built in the shape of Sri Lanka’s national flower, the lotus. While the tower isn’t functional yet, you can still go up close and get some great pictures of this iconic building..


To Lotus Tower – 10 minutes drive

INDEPENDENCE SQUARE

A MONUMENT OF INDEPENDENCE


The Independence Square was built to commemorate the formal ceremony of Sri Lanka’s independence from British colonial rule on 4th February 1948. Located in Cinnamon Gardens, Independence Square is located approximately 3 km away from Granbell Hotel.


The open-sided hall is adorned with beautifully carved columns and a tiled roof that was inspired by the last Kandyan Kingdom. There are several statues of seated lions surrounding the hall too. You can also visit the Memorial Museum located below the Memorial Hall.


The manicured gardens around the Memorial Hall are often used by locals for recreational activities in the mornings and evenings.


To Independence Square – 7 minutes drive
